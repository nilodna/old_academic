About Me

I am a Brazilian oceanographer, currently developing a Ph.D. research at the Oceanographic Institute of the University of São Paulo, Brazil. My main interests focus on coastal and shelf processes and my current work is combined effort of modeling/observational approaches to investigate high frequency heat flux variability in coastal waters. I'm also interested in low-budget oceanographic instrumentation and a truly passionate about python's power over marine science. During my master's research, I use numerical modeling combined with in situ observations to explore how atmospheric blocking episodes can affect the continental shelf circulation. Check the full dissertation here (in brazilian portuguese).
